# Bobby's Discount Store — Signature

## The one concept
**"The Everything Counter."** A humble Main Street variety store treated like a
curated department-store directory. The brand truth (a regular's review: "this is
the first place I go and almost always find what I need") meets a modern-lux
editorial art direction it has never been given before.

## The signature moment
The hero **word-cycle**: a single headline reads *"If you need ___, it's probably
here."* The blank rotates through real things people actually run out of (a phone
charger, fresh towels, cleaning supplies, party balloons, AA batteries, a new
broom, storage bins, school stuff), each rising in and out in warm tan. It turns
the store's entire value proposition (one stop, you'll find it) into the page's
motion. One confident move, restraint everywhere else.

## Locked divergence lane (honored)
- **Accent:** #D4A574 warm tan/gold (full token ramp tan → rust for emphasis).
- **Display font:** Cabinet Grotesk via Fontshare (loaded through Fontshare CSS

  icon at 375px.
- **Mood:** modern-lux. Warm ivory paper, faint grain, dark-ink "Why" section for
  contrast, generous whitespace, hairline tan rules.

## Why it reads as THIS business
Real category imagery, each shot used once and matched to its label: a smiling
shopper with an orange basket in a packed corner store (community-true to the City
of Orange), a stocked floor-to-ceiling aisle, cookware, detergents, folded towels,
health-and-beauty shelves, a party sparkler, hardware stock. It is unmistakably a
discount/variety store, never "could be anything."
