# Bobby's Discount Store — External Reference (Blocklist Law: external winners only)

Anchored to external award-grade reference, not any past internal build.

## Studied direction (retail / general-store / editorial commerce, ~last 18 mo)
- **Awwwards e-commerce + retail collections** — the recurring winning move for
  general/variety retail is to treat an ordinary catalog as an *editorial
  directory*: oversized grotesk type, numbered sections, generous whitespace, one
  kinetic headline, restrained palette. Borrowed the *bar* (editorial calm + one
  motion idea), not any shape.
- **Interstore / retail-design nominees** — confident left-aligned index layouts
  with a persistent rail; informed the locked left-rail nav being treated as a
  table-of-contents, not chrome.
- **"Walk the aisle" retail concepts** — a known award pattern is making the site
  feel like moving through the store. Reinterpreted abstractly via the marquee of
  real SKUs and the word-cycle of real needs, rather than a literal 3D aisle.

## How this build differs from the category cliché
Discount/variety stores online default to: cluttered yellow-red "SALE" sigsigns,
clip-art price bursts, stock-template grids, no art direction. This build rejects
all of that for a quiet, expensive, modern-lux editorial treatment in warm tan and
ivory. A visitor has not seen a corner discount store presented this way.

## Signature derivation
Craft verb of a variety store = *"find."* The site dramatizes finding: the hero
word-cycle resolves the customer's errand into "it's probably here," and the
directory maps the floor before they walk in.
